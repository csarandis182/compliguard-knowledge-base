# TGA Advertising Code 2021 — Part 8: Restricted Representations

**Source:** Therapeutic Goods (Therapeutic Goods Advertising Code) Instrument 2021  
**Compilation:** No. 1 — 20 December 2022  
**chunk_id:** tga-part8-restricted-representations

---

## Overview

Part 8 defines what constitutes a **"serious form"** of a disease, condition, ailment or defect, and sets out the **public interest criteria** for the TGA Secretary when deciding whether to approve restricted representations. Restricted representations require prior approval from the TGA Secretary under section 42DF of the Act before use.

> **Critical compliance note:** Advertising a therapeutic good for a condition that qualifies as "serious" under this Part — without prior TGA approval — is an offence under sections 42DL and 42DLB of the Act. Penalties apply.

---

## Section 28 — Restricted Representations: Definition of "Serious Form"

For the purposes of section 42DD of the Act, a form of a disease, condition, ailment or defect is a **serious form** if:

- **(a)** it is medically accepted that the form **requires diagnosis or treatment or supervision by a suitably qualified health practitioner**, except where the form has been medically diagnosed and medically accepted as being suitable for self-treatment and management; or
- **(b)** there is a **diagnostic (including screening), preventative, monitoring, susceptibility or pre-disposition test** available for the form (including a self-administered test), which **requires medical interpretation or follow-up.**

### Exclusion

The following is **not** a serious form:
- **(c)** pregnancy, other than pregnancy with a medical, obstetric or surgical complication.

### Examples of Conditions Likely Qualifying as Serious Forms

The following conditions are generally considered to require health practitioner supervision and would typically be classified as serious forms (requiring TGA approval for restricted representation use):

- Cancer / oncology conditions
- Cardiovascular disease (heart disease, hypertension)
- Diabetes
- Autoimmune conditions (multiple sclerosis, rheumatoid arthritis, lupus)
- Mental health conditions (depression, anxiety disorders, schizophrenia)
- Neurological conditions (Alzheimer's, Parkinson's)
- Chronic kidney or liver disease
- HIV/AIDS
- Serious infections requiring medical treatment

> **Note:** The TGA publishes guidance on what constitutes a restricted representation. Advertisers uncertain whether a condition qualifies as "serious" should seek TGA pre-approval or legal advice before publishing.

## Section 29 — Public Interest Criteria for Restricted Representation Approval

For the purposes of paragraph 42DF(4)(c) of the Act, the public interest criteria the TGA Secretary must consider when deciding whether to approve a restricted representation are:

- **(a)** whether the representation would be likely to **exploit, or take advantage of, vulnerable consumers**, or particular groups of consumers, impacted by the disease, condition, ailment or defect; and
- **(b)** whether the representation would be likely to **delay or discourage consumers from seeking timely medical attention**, where the attention is necessary to prevent negative health consequences, morbidity, mortality, or deterioration or progression of the condition; and
- **(c)** whether the representation (itself or together with other representations) would be likely to have a **negative impact on public health**; and
- **(d)** any other public interest criteria that the Secretary considers relevant.

---

## How Restricted Representations Work in Practice

A **restricted representation** is a claim in an advertisement that a therapeutic good is related to a serious form of disease. Under section 42DD of the Act, such claims are prohibited unless:

1. The TGA Secretary has granted **prior written approval** under section 42DF; or
2. A **permission** has been granted under section 42DK; or
3. The representation is a warning or contra-indication **required by a legislative instrument** to be included in an advertisement (this can be used without prior approval).

### Process for Obtaining Approval

Advertisers seeking to use a restricted representation must apply to the TGA for approval. The application is assessed against the public interest criteria in section 29 above.

---

## Compliance Checklist for Restricted Representations

- [ ] Does the advertisement reference a disease, condition, ailment or defect?
- [ ] If yes: Is that condition a "serious form" under s.28 (i.e., requires health practitioner supervision)?
- [ ] If yes: Has TGA prior written approval been obtained under s.42DF?
- [ ] If no approval: The claim must be removed or modified to avoid making a restricted representation.
