# TGA Advertising Code 2021 — Part 3: General Requirements

**Source:** Therapeutic Goods (Therapeutic Goods Advertising Code) Instrument 2021  
**Compilation:** No. 1 — 20 December 2022  
**chunk_id:** tga-part3-general-requirements

---

## Overview

Part 3 specifies the general requirements for all advertisements about therapeutic goods to ensure they are accurate, balanced, not misleading, promote safe use, and are consistent with public health campaigns.

---

## Section 8 — Accuracy

1. An advertisement about therapeutic goods must:
   - **(a) be accurate, balanced and not misleading or likely to be misleading; and**
   - **(b) only contain information that is substantiated by the advertiser prior to publication or dissemination.**

2. An advertisement about therapeutic goods included in the Register must not be **inconsistent with any indication or intended purpose accepted** in relation to the inclusion of the goods in the Register.

> **Compliance note:** Every claim must be substantiated before the ad is published. Post-publication substantiation is not compliant.

---

## Section 9 — Safe and Proper Use

### 9(1) — Prohibited representations about safety/efficacy

An advertisement must not contain any statement, pictorial representation or design that, expressly or by implication, represents the goods to be:
- **(a) safe, or without harm or side-effects; or**
- **(b) effective in all cases, or a guaranteed cure; or**
- **(c) infallible, unfailing, magical or miraculous.**

### 9(2) — Prohibited emotional manipulations

An advertisement must not:
- **(a) cause, or be likely to cause, undue alarm, fear or distress; or**
- **(b) contain a representation to the effect that harmful consequences may result from the therapeutic goods not being used,** unless the representation has prior approval under section 42DF or permission under section 42DK of the Act.

### 9(3) — Prohibited misleading representations

An advertisement must not contain any statement, pictorial representation or design that, expressly or by implication:
- **(a)** is inconsistent with the label, directions for use, consumer medicine information, instructions for use, or patient information leaflet of the goods; or
- **(b) delays or discourages, or is likely to delay or discourage, persons from seeking necessary medical attention; or**
- **(c) delays or discourages, or is likely to delay or discourage, persons from undertaking treatment prescribed by a medical practitioner; or**
- **(d) exaggerates, or is likely to exaggerate, the efficacy or performance of the goods; or**
- **(e) encourages, or is likely to encourage, inappropriate or excessive use of the goods; or**
- **(f)** compares the goods with other therapeutic goods, classes of therapeutic goods, or therapeutic services where such comparison **suggests that the comparator goods or services are harmful or ineffectual.**

---

## Section 10 — Consistency with Public Health Campaigns

An advertisement about therapeutic goods **must not be inconsistent with a current public health campaign.**

---

## Section 11 — Scientific or Clinical Representations

> Note: This section does not apply to labels, consumer medicine information, instructions for use, or patient information leaflets.

### 11(2) — Requirements for scientific/clinical claims

An advertisement that makes a scientific or clinical representation must:
- **(a) only contain scientific or clinical terminology that is clearly communicated and able to be readily understood** by the audience to whom it is directed; and
- **(b) be consistent with the body of scientific or clinical evidence** applicable to the goods.

### 11(3) — Requirements when referencing research

An advertisement that refers to scientific or clinical research, expressly or by implication, must:
- **(a) identify the researcher; and**
- **(b) identify the financial sponsor of the research** where the advertiser knows, or ought reasonably to have known, that information; and
- **(c) sufficiently identify the research by proper citation** to enable consumers to access that research.

> **Compliance note:** Vague references such as "studies show" or "clinically tested" without a citation are non-compliant under 11(3)(c).

## Section 12 — Advertising to Children

> Note: Does not apply to labels of therapeutic goods.

1. An advertisement about therapeutic goods **must not be directed to children aged under 12 years.**

2. An advertisement may only be directed to children aged 12 years and over if:
   - **(a)(i)** the goods are mentioned in an item in **Annexure 1**; and
   - **(a)(ii)** the conditions (if any) for that item are met; and
   - **(b)** the goods do not contain a substance included in Schedule 2, 3, 4 or 8 to the current Poisons Standard.

| 3 | Class I medical devices for the management of chronic conditions under medical supervision |
| 4 | Cold sore preparations |
| 5 | Condoms and personal lubricants |
| 6 | Face masks and gloves for preventing transmission of diseases |
| 7 | Hand sanitisers |
| 8 | Lip balm |
| 9 | Oral hygiene products, including toothpaste, mouthwash and interdental brushes |

| 13 | Wound care dressings for superficial wounds, including first aid items and antiseptics |
