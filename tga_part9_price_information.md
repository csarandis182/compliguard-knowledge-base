# TGA Advertising Code 2021 — Part 9: Price Information

**Source:** Therapeutic Goods (Therapeutic Goods Advertising Code) Instrument 2021  
**Compilation:** No. 1 — 20 December 2022  
**chunk_id:** tga-part9-price-information

---

## Overview

Part 9 sets out the conditions under which **information about the price of prescription medicines and certain pharmacist-only medicines** (registered goods containing Schedule 3, 4 or 8 substances) may be provided to the general public.

> **Note:** Part 9 is the only part of the TGA Advertising Code that applies to advertisements consisting solely of price information for Schedule 3/4/8 medicines. All other parts of the Code do not apply to such advertisements.

---

## Section 32 — Who May Publish Price Information

Price information may **only** be published or disseminated by:
- A **retail pharmacy**
- An **agent acting on behalf of a retail pharmacy** (including a pharmacy marketing group)
- A **medical practitioner** approved under section 92 of the National Health Act 1953

---

## Section 32 — Prohibited Publication Channels

Price information must **not** be published or disseminated via:
- Radio or television transmission (including pay and streaming services)
- Digital or non-digital displays in shopping malls outside individual pharmacies
- Displays in or on public transport
- Billboards
- Cinema advertising

---

## Section 33 — Presentation of Price Information

Price information in relation to a medicine must be:
- **(a)** published or disseminated in a **list containing not less than 25 medicines** (the "price information list"); and
- **(b)** accompanied by the name and contact details of the retail supplier from whom each medicine may be purchased at the list price.

The price information list must specify medicines in **alphabetical order** by trade name or active ingredient (subject to schedule grouping exceptions).

---

## Section 34 — Description of Medicines in Price Lists

Each medicine in a price information list must be specified with:
- The name of the medicine (TGO 91 or TGO 92)
- If more than one strength — the strength of each active ingredient as on the label
- The dosage form
- The purchase price of the relevant number of units of the standard pack
- The quantity contained in the standard pack

---

## Section 35 — General Restrictions on Price Lists

A price information list must NOT:
- **(a)** Present or describe a medicine in a way that **directs consumers to a particular medicine** over any other
- **(b)** Be accompanied by **any promotional statement, pictorial representation or design**
- **(c)** Include: an adjective or phrase that qualifies the name, formulation or pack size; a term indicating predicted or recommended length of supply; or any embellishment
- **(d)** Promote quantities or multiple pack sizes not accepted in relation to the inclusion of the goods in the Register
- **(e)** Use a **comparative adjective or term** to qualify the purchase price (e.g. "cheapest in town")
- **(f)** Give any **prominence to a particular medicine** compared to any other in the list
- **(g)** Offer **rewards or bonus points**, or be associated with advertising that promotes rewards/bonus points
- **(h)** Limit or qualify the availability of the price (other than a statement of validity/expiration)
- **(i)** Be accompanied by, or located in proximity to, information about **indications, diseases, conditions, ailments or defects** so that a reasonable person might infer the medicine would cure or alleviate those conditions

---

## Section 36 — PBS-Listed Medicines

If a pharmacy marketing group's price list includes a PBS-listed medicine that carries a brand or therapeutic group premium, and the list includes the pharmacy's own generic of that medicine, the list **must also include price information for at least one other bench-mark price brand** of that medicine (where one exists).

Price lists including PBS medicines must clearly:
- Indicate that the medicine is listed in the PBS
- Specify the total purchase price with reference to the general price or concessional price or both
- Indicate that the purchase price is subsidised by the Australian Government and only applies when the medicine is prescribed for the medical conditions listed in the PBS schedule
